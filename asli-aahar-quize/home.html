Organic Food v/s Inorganic Food

Organic foods are produced using processes without synthetics such as chemical fertilizers and pesticides. Inorganic foods, on the other hand, use synthetics to produce food products.

Organic Food

  • Organic food is healthier due to the use of natural fertilisers for plant growth
  • Organic food tastes better
  • Organic foods are not genetically modified
  • Organic food does not cause any harmful environmental impacts

Inorganic Food

  • Inorganic food is not so healthy for the body due to the use of synthetic fertilisers for plant growth
  • Inorganic food is less tasty when compared to organic food
  • Inorganic foods look vibrant and more visually appealing
  • Inorganic food causes harmful environmental impacts

Organic farming done using sustainable agricultural practices like drip irrigation, rainwater harvesting pits, use of biopesticides and eliminating middlemen to generate prosperity for the consumer and farmer is only possible when done on a large scale as opposed to a single farmer unit.

Hence, inorganic farming practices like the use of pesticides for faster plant growth, borewell irrigation as opposed to drip irrigation etc. can be done even by a single farming unit which results in a good amount of yield and profit.

Innovative ways by which
InAcres is growing organic food

To be a part of the organic food movement, InAcres, a passionate sustainable farm management unit, is working towards building communities that grow and eat food together. It actively manages and develops farms through Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) practice. Over the past couple of years, the unit has made significant changes to the ecosystems of Shadnagar and Bhoothpur. The unique and sustainable methods InAcres is using as a part of its farming methods are,

  • Use of Biopesticides
  • Use of seeds from the parent plant to grow the next generation plant
  • Use of cattle urine and dung infused water
  • Use of jute sheets for mulching to preserve topsoil fertility
